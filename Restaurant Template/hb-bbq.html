Our Story

Meet the Pitmaster.
Kenyon Railey.

Before the sun rises over Dallas, Kenyon Railey is already at the pit. Splitting Post Oak, building fires, and loading the offset smoker the same way he has every morning since 2018. For Kenyon, barbecue isn't a business — it's an obsession with getting every detail right.

He chases the perfect bark — that deep, almost-black crust where pepper, smoke, and rendered fat fuse into something you can't fake. Every brisket is hand-trimmed, seasoned with nothing but coarse black pepper and kosher salt, then set on the grate at 225 degrees. No wrapping early. No shortcuts. Just clean Post Oak smoke, patience, and 14 hours of unwavering attention.

It's Texas tradition, elevated by a pitmaster who refuses to cut a single corner.

01 Select Hand-picked USDA Prime and Choice cuts, trimmed by hand.
02 Season Coarse black pepper, kosher salt. Nothing more, nothing less.
03 Smoke 14 hours over Post Oak at 225°. Low, slow, and unwavering.

"We don't rush the smoke.
The smoke doesn't rush us."

— Kenyon Railey, Pitmaster & Founder
